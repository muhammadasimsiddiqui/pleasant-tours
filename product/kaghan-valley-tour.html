Kaghan Valley Tour

Embark on a 7-day journey starting from Karachi, exploring Pakistan's stunning Kaghan Valley, cultural treasures, and vibrant cities, culminating in unforgettable natural and historical experiences.

Days: 7

Person:
Total Price: 25,000Pkr

Description:

Nestled within Khyber Pakhtunkhwa, Pakistan, Kaghan Valley beckons with its captivating natural allure. Naran, a gateway to its treasures, unveils vistas of snow-clad peaks, notably the majestic Malika Parbat. Lake Saiful Muluk, a legendary alpine jewel surrounded by towering mountains, enchants with its azure waters and picturesque backdrop. The Babusar Pass, at 4,173 meters, offers a breathtaking gateway to Gilgit-Baltistan, presenting panoramic views and a thrilling passage through the Karakoram Mountains. Kaghan Valley is a haven for nature enthusiasts, boasting lush meadows, cascading waterfalls, and mystical forests. Adventurers revel in trekking, hiking, and camping, embracing the embrace of nature. A diverse wildlife, including the endangered Himalayan snow leopard, completes this symphony of natural wonders, ensuring Kaghan Valley promises an unforgettable rendezvous with nature’s grandeur.


Start your adventure with a flight from Karachi to Islamabad. Upon arrival, enjoy a brief city tour, visiting prominent landmarks such as Faisal Mosque and Daman-e-Koh, which offers panoramic views of the city. Spend the night in Islamabad, preparing for the journey ahead.


Depart from Islamabad and embark on a picturesque drive to Naran. Pass through charming towns like Hassan Abdal, Haripur, and Abbottabad, soaking in the stunning vistas along the way. Stop in Mansehra for a delicious lunch and a short rest. Continue through Balakot, the gateway to Kaghan Valley, and arrive in Naran by evening. Enjoy the serene ambiance and natural beauty of Naran. Overnight stay in Naran.


Embarking on our journey alongside the picturesque River Kunhar, we ascend towards the enchanting Lulusar Lake. Passing through the idyllic villages of Batakundi, Jalkhad, and Burwai, we immerse ourselves in the breathtaking landscapes that surround us. Lulusar Lake, with its glistening waters, awaits like a shimmering gem, a photographer’s dream. Continuing our expedition, we traverse Gitidas, a charming summer enclave, before reaching the majestic Babusar Top at an elevation of 4173 meters. Here, we pause to absorb the breathtaking panoramas before descending back to Naran. Amidst this scenic haven, we savor the memories of our journey, enveloped in the serene ambiance of nature’s beauty. Overnight Naran.


We embark on an exciting adventure to Saif-ul-Muluk Lake, a legendary and breathtakingly beautiful lake nestled at an elevation of 3200 meters. The presence of Malika Parbat, the highest peak in the valley, enhances the charm of the surroundings, perfect for capturing stunning photographs. We have various transportation options, including jeeps, walking, or riding ponies, to reach the lake, immersing ourselves in the natural splendor along the way. Enjoying a packed picnic lunch amidst the serene surroundings rejuvenates us for the rest of the day’s activities. In the afternoon, we experience the thrill of rafting in the gushing waters of River Kunhar, adding an adrenaline rush to our journey. As the day comes to an end, we return to Naran, cherishing the unforgettable memories of the mesmerizing experiences we’ve had. Overnight Naran.


Drive from Naran to Shogran, a scenic route offering lush green valleys and snow-capped peaks. Visit Sri Paye, a high-altitude meadow with panoramic mountain views. Take your time to explore and soak in the tranquility of this enchanting place. Overnight stay in Shogran.


Depart from Shogran and drive back to Islamabad. Enjoy the scenic journey and reflect on the incredible experiences and memories gathered during your time in the mountains. Spend the night in Islamabad, savoring your last night in the capital.


In the morning, take a city tour of Islamabad, visiting the Lok Virsa Museum and Rawal Lake. Later, catch a flight back to Karachi, concluding your journey with a deep appreciation for Pakistan's natural beauty and cultural richness.
